# Government & Law Enforcement Data Request Policy

**Entity:** Rushford Collyer LLC ("the Company"), operator of PostPilot
**Address:** 2108 N ST STE N, Sacramento, CA 95816
**Effective date:** July 6, 2026
**Policy owner:** Jeremy Collyer, Member — privacy@postpilot.app

## 1. Purpose and scope

This policy governs how Rushford Collyer LLC responds to requests from public authorities — law enforcement, government agencies, courts, or regulators — for the personal data or personal information of PostPilot users, including any Platform Data obtained through Meta's APIs (Instagram, and any other connected platforms). It applies to every member, employee, and contractor of the Company. No one may disclose user data to a public authority except in accordance with this policy.

## 2. Legality review (required)

Every request is reviewed for legal validity **before** any data is disclosed. The Company confirms that:

- the request comes through valid legal process (e.g., subpoena, court order, search warrant, or a lawful statutory demand);
- the requesting authority has jurisdiction over the Company and the data;
- the request is specific, and identifies the user and data sought; and
- disclosure is actually required or permitted by applicable law.

If the request is informal, unclear, or lacks proper legal process, the Company does not disclose data and requests proper process. Where the legality is uncertain, the Company consults legal counsel before responding.

## 3. Challenging unlawful or overbroad requests

The Company will object to, seek to narrow, or formally challenge any request it reasonably believes is unlawful, invalid, overbroad, or improper — including by requiring proper legal process, filing objections, seeking to quash or limit the request, and engaging legal counsel as needed. The Company will not comply with a request it believes to be unlawful without first exhausting reasonable means to challenge it.

## 4. Data minimization

If the Company is legally required to disclose data, it discloses only the **minimum information necessary** to satisfy the specific, valid request. The Company does not provide data beyond the scope of the request, does not volunteer additional user data, and prefers the least-sensitive responsive data available.

## 5. Documentation and recordkeeping

The Company keeps a written record of every public-authority request, retained securely and separately from production systems. Each record includes:

- the requesting authority and the individuals involved (both external and internal);
- the date, legal basis, and full text or copy of the request;
- the legal reasoning applied, including any legality review, objections, or challenges;
- what data (if any) was disclosed, and the date and scope of disclosure; and
- any notice given to the affected user.

Records are retained for at least the period required by applicable law.

## 6. User notification

Where permitted by law and not prohibited by a valid legal order (e.g., a non-disclosure or gag provision), the Company notifies affected users of a request for their data before disclosing it, so they have an opportunity to respond or object.

## 7. Emergency requests

For bona fide emergencies involving an imminent risk of serious harm or death, the Company may expedite review but still verifies the requesting authority and discloses only the minimum data necessary, and documents the request per Section 5.

## 8. Point of responsibility and review

All public-authority requests must be routed immediately to the policy owner (privacy@postpilot.app), who is responsible for applying this policy. This policy is reviewed at least annually and updated as laws or Company practices change.
